# BoredRoom Product Context

## Register

Product. The public landing page is also a high-expression brand surface; gameplay surfaces prioritize clarity while retaining the same visual identity.

## Users

BoredRoom is for Nigerian friends, families, and coworkers playing party games together in one physical room. Desktop and TV devices host the public display, phones act as private controllers, and tablets can act as controllers or optional host companions.

## Product Purpose

One house session should carry a group through an entire game night. Players join once, the host selects installed games, controllers switch automatically, and the session records round and overall winners without exposing private state.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, playful, theatrical, distinctly Nigerian, and social. The Lagos night setting should feel recognisable and alive without competing with controls or game state.

## Anti-references

- Sterile corporate dashboards or generic SaaS panels.
- Separate operator, room, pack, or secondary-code flows.
- Oversized or repetitive space effects that make the sky feel artificial.
- Decorative motion that obscures gameplay, text, or touch targets.
- Game screens that abandon the shared BoredRoom visual language.

## Design Principles

- One room and one code for the whole night.
- The public display is the stage; controllers are private, direct, and thumb-friendly.
- Lagos artwork anchors the product, while game state remains the visual priority.
- Motion adds atmosphere and feedback, never delay or ambiguity.
- Device roles and route behavior are explicit and predictable.
- Installed games retain their identity inside consistent BoredRoom chrome.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA contrast where practical, preserve keyboard operation on host surfaces, use touch targets of at least 44 CSS pixels, respect safe areas and reduced-motion preferences, announce connection and turn changes to assistive technology, and never rely on color alone to communicate legal moves or status.
